WHAT WE SELL
============

This document describes the product and value proposition of the chatbot platform. The customer-facing name in the app is IndusCart — replace with your go-to-market name if you white-label.


ONE-LINE PITCH
--------------

AI-assisted customer care for Indian SMEs — shoppers get answers on WhatsApp and your website widget; your team runs everything from one workspace console, with human handoff when it matters.


WHO IT IS FOR
-------------

- Small and mid-sized businesses in India (and similar markets) that sell, support, or book over WhatsApp and the web.

- Agencies or multi-brand operators that manage several "clients" (brands / stores) in one workspace — each with its own knowledge, widget ID, and optional WhatsApp setup.

- Merchants who need a sandboxed portal to see only their business: conversations, knowledge uploads, and thread status — without full admin access.


WHAT CUSTOMERS BUY (CAPABILITIES)
---------------------------------

Channels

- Website chat widget — one script tag (widget.js) with a public ID per client; visitors chat in the browser; messages hit your hosted API with CORS for merchant sites.

- WhatsApp Business (Cloud API) — inbound webhooks and outbound replies when Meta credentials are configured per client.

AI and knowledge

- RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) — answers are grounded in documents you upload (e.g. PDFs, policies, FAQs) per client, using embeddings and vector search — not a generic model-only chatbot.

- Reply languages — including English, Hindi, Hinglish, and "match customer's language" for the widget where configured.

- Conversation memory for automation — prior thread messages (including team replies marked in-model as [Team]) are passed into the next AI turn so the assistant stays consistent after a human steps in.

Team workflow

- Shared inbox — conversations across channels, with status, handoff flags, and confidence signals.

- Staff replies in-thread — team members can send messages from the conversation detail screen; website widget visitors see them via polling; WhatsApp customers get the same text on-thread when a phone number and Cloud API are on file.

- Handoff and alerts — when the model is unsure or policy requires it, conversations can be flagged for humans; hooks exist for notifying the team (e.g. email via SES when configured).

Operations and access

- Workspace — org-level settings, team, automations (org-wide toggles), analytics summary, retention and AI settings where implemented.

- Merchant portal — optional access per login via client_portal_access: scoped routes and APIs so a business owner sees only their allowed clients.

- Security posture — portal vs full workspace separation; public widget APIs validated by publicId + visitor/session linkage; staff APIs require authentication.

What we are NOT positioning as shipped revenue features

Some UI areas are roadmap or placeholder (e.g. payments gateway, full billing portal). Treat those as future or custom implementation unless you have wired them for a specific launch.


DEPLOYMENT SHAPE (HOW WE SELL IT TECHNICALLY)
--------------------------------------------

- SaaS-style hosting — e.g. Vercel + managed Postgres (Supabase) with pgvector, Next.js App Router, Drizzle ORM.

- Bring your own keys — OpenAI for chat/embeddings; Meta for WhatsApp; optional AWS for email and related services per .env.example.
